## 1. CHRONOLOGICAL DISTILLATES (Key Years)

---

### 2010 -- "Technological Messianism"
*Data basis: GA_2010_AH, 17 data points*

1. Humanity stands at a historic turning point where it first develops the tools to actively direct its own evolution -- this transformation is inevitable but its direction is shapeable.
2. True progress emerges not from incremental improvement but from disruptive revolution: The most important technologies begin as derided playthings at the margin.
3. The human is not an endpoint but a transitional stage -- biological limitations like limited intelligence, language-bound communication, and mortality are temporary technological constraints that can be overcome.
4. Whoever controls the leading AI systems decides whether the future will be free or totalitarian -- technology development is a moral and political obligation.
5. Transformative technology must be simultaneously rooted in daily life (as obvious as brushing teeth) and carry potential to be humanity's greatest contribution in centuries.

**Key concepts:** Disruption, telepathy, 300-year vision, post-humanism, freedom vs. totalitarianism

---

### 2013 -- "Moral Obligation to Innovate"
*Data basis: GA_2013_AH, 39 data points*

1. Technological progress is not merely desirable but morally imperative -- every delay in developing life-saving technologies prolongs preventable suffering and is unethical.
2. The world is better than media portrays it -- optimism is not naivete but more accurate representation of reality, and only who believes in better future dares take great risks.
3. The smartest and most resourced people have not only capability but obligation to pursue radically transformative "moonshots," even when most say they're impossible.
4. Technology must democratize capabilities and knowledge: Deep learning gives people without domain expertise powerful tools, online courses open world-class education access globally.
5. We must stay vigilant -- technology can be misused and ethical boundaries are necessary: no autonomous weapons, no privacy violation, no truth subordination to special interests.

**Key concepts:** Moonshots, life extension, democratization, media negativity, elite responsibility

---

### 2016 -- "Acceleration as Moral Imperative"
*Data basis: GA_2016_AH, 70 data points*

1. Technology is the only force that can solve fundamental human problems -- including disease, aging, and death -- and we stand at threshold of transformative breakthroughs in AI, biotech, and brain-computer interfaces.
2. Speed is a moral imperative: Every year of delay costs human lives, so one must be ready to move fast, take risks, and be misunderstood for years.
3. Innovation comes from bold individuals and startups at margins, not governments; private actors with skin in the game make better decisions than bureaucrats.
4. We're in an existential race -- with other nations, with time, with stagnation risks -- and whoever gains control over data and AI development determines humanity's future.
5. The greatest danger is not moving too fast but becoming too slow from fear and missing the historic chance to transcend humanity's biological boundaries.

**Key concepts:** AlphaGo, Death-is-a-bug, day-1 mentality, libertarianism, stagnation-as-risk

---

### 2019 -- "Human-Centered AI and Institutional Awakening"
*Data basis: GA_2019_AH, 85 data points*

1. Artificial intelligence is the most transformative technology in human history, potentially leading to superintelligent systems and already having economic, political, and existential dimensions.
2. The standard AI model -- programming machines with fixed objective functions -- is dangerous engineering error; instead, systems must be developed that are inherently uncertain about human values and actively learn them.
3. AI development cannot be left to technologists alone, but requires interdisciplinary collaboration and hybrid institutional structures combining academic independence, industrial resources, and democratic control.
4. The geopolitical competition for AI dominance between democratic and authoritarian systems is existential -- whoever controls AI controls freedom's or totalitarianism's future.
5. The future is shapeable, not determined: Whether AI leads to broad prosperity or mass unemployment depends on institutional, ethical, and political decisions we make today.

**Key concepts:** Human-centered AI, value alignment, OECD principles, Cambridge Analytica, interdisciplinarity

---

### 2022 -- "Stochastic Parrots and Cosmic Awakening"
*Data basis: GA_2022_AH, 155 data points*

1. Artificial intelligence is not merely technology but direct path to humanity's deepest questions about consciousness, intelligence, and reality's nature -- 2022 marks turning point from theoretical speculation to practical power.
2. Scaling produces emergent capabilities we can't fully predict, which is why we simultaneously need maximum development speed and maximum safety research (RLHF, Constitutional AI, mechanistic interpretability).
3. Power concentration -- whether in tech monopolies, authoritarian governments, or "finance gerontocracies" -- is central danger, which is why technological decentralization (Web3, crypto, open source) is strategic necessity.
4. Humanity has unique cosmic role -- we are the method by which the universe knows itself -- and with AI, we potentiate or delegate this capability to new forms of consciousness.
5. Speed is decisive because innovation comes in unequal waves and we're in a "special moment of tech history" -- whoever doesn't act now misses opportunity to shape the future.

**Key concepts:** ChatGPT, emergence, scaling laws, stochastic parrot, Web3, Ukraine, cosmic humanism

---

### 2025 -- "Maniacal Urgency and the Decisive Decade"
*Data basis: GA_2025_AH, 500 data points*

1. We live in the decisive decade of human history when AGI shifts from science fiction to empirical reality -- with timelines compressed from 2050 to 2027-2028, driven by exponential capability increases.
2. This transformation is a geopolitical race between USA and China where winner is determined by market share and American dominance is equated with higher probability for humanistic superintelligence.
3. The only sustainable competitive advantage is extreme execution speed -- manifested as ascetic obsession, 24/7 work ethic, and ability to achieve supercomputers in 19 days and $100M ARR in 12 months.
4. Existential risks (25% p(doom), recursive self-improvement from 2027-2030) are acknowledged but resolved through logic that slowness is more dangerous than speed -- safety through ownership, not regulation.
5. The 2035 vision is democratized access to "unlimited genius" for every human, with human economy transformed -- humans as orchestrators of AI agents rather than executors.

**Key concepts:** AGI in 2-3 years, p(doom), Stargate, execution-is-the-moat, manager-nerds, techno-nationalism

## 5. ANALYSIS: THE MOVING ELEMENTS

**What changes between 2010 and 2025?**

### Movement B1: From Abstract Optimism to Bimodal Future Vision
- **2010-2013:** Nearly unbroken progress optimism (D06=9-10). Risks mentioned but seen as manageable.
- **2016-2019:** Growing ambivalence. AI safety becomes independent field. "I'm optimistic, but not naive."
- **2022-2025:** Bimodal future expectation: Either radical boom OR catastrophe. "There is no middle ground." (D06 falls from 9 to 6-7)

### Movement B2: From Impatience to Existential Panic
- **2010:** Ambitious impatience ("build faster"), but no existential urgency. (D08=7)
- **2016:** "Move fast" becomes moral imperative. (D08=10)
- **2022:** "Special moment in tech history" -- mass exodus from major labs to startups. (D08=10)
- **2025:** "Maniacal sense of urgency," 24/7 work, 30-pound weight gain, "violence of velocity." (D08=10)

### Movement B3: From Libertarianism to Techno-Nationalism
- **2010-2013:** Libertarian foundation. Freedom vs. totalitarianism is abstract-philosophical.
- **2016:** Political disruption (Trump support as "tool"). First explicit China references.
- **2019:** "AI is a military technology." National AI strategies. OECD principles.
- **2025:** Open techno-nationalism. "America must win." $500B Stargate. Export controls. Pentagon contracts.

### Movement B6: From "Human as Transitional Stage" to "Human as Valuable Orchestrator"

- **2016:** "Brain-computer interfaces as symbiosis." Human merges with technology.

### Movement B7: From AGI-as-Distant-Goal to AGI-as-Near-Expectation

## 7. META-DISTILLATE: The AI Elite's Overall Worldview 2010-2026 in 5 Sentences

Based on all read syntheses, all epoch ratings, and all identified constants and movements, the collective worldview of global AI elite can be distilled into five sentences:

---

> **Sentence 1 -- Inevitability:**
> Artificial intelligence is the most profound transformation in human history -- its development follows its own laws (scaling laws, emergence, exponential acceleration), is stoppable by no force on Earth, and will within years produce systems surpassing human intelligence in every dimension.

> **Sentence 2 -- The Mission:**
> A small elite of brilliant, obsessively working technologists bears historic responsibility to shape this transformation because democratic institutions are too slow, governments too ignorant, and the population too uninformed -- speed, risk-taking, and ability to be misunderstood for years are not weaknesses but central virtues.

> **Sentence 3 -- The Duality:**
> AI is simultaneously greatest promise (cure disease, end poverty, extend humanity) and greatest threat (existential risk, total surveillance, human obsolescence) -- this ambivalence is not contradiction but a work assignment, where solution lies in more and better technology, never in stopping or slowing.

> **Sentence 4 -- The Race:**
> The geopolitical struggle for AI dominance between democratic and authoritarian systems -- concretely: between USA and China -- is existential zero-sum competition where technological dominance determines humanity's fate and where every acceleration is justified because the alternative ("the wrong ones" winning) is worse than any acceleration risk.

> **Sentence 5 -- Human Transformation:**
> The human in biological form is a limited but noble transitional being -- the "biological bootloader" of artificial intelligence -- whose future lies not in competing with machines but in symbiosis: as orchestrator of AI agents, as ethical compass for superintelligence, and ultimately as species transcending itself to build civilization larger than anything a single biological brain could imagine.

## METHODOLOGICAL NOTE

This document extracts from 8 synthetic worldview reconstructions (SWR) each the STEP-3 section ("Core Beliefs") and condenses the results into comparable 5-sentence distillates. The analysis rests additionally on:
- Rating data from `rating_epochen_D01-D12.md` (4 epochs, 12 dimensions)
- Time series data from `zeitreihen_daten.json` (17 years, 12 dimensions)
- 3 epoch syntheses (10-16, 16-22, 23-26) with own reflection sections

Identification of "constants" (K1-K5) and "movements" (B1-B7) occurs through systematic comparison of all distillates. A belief counts as "constant" if it appears in all 8 syntheses and all 3 epochs. A "movement" occurs when dimension changes significantly across epochs (delta >= 2 points on 10-scale or qualitative shift in formulation).
