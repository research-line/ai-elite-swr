# Memo Phase 2: Initial Observations from Sample Review

**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Sample:** 100 statements + 60 actions (5 statements + 3 actions per person, Top 20)
**Method:** Open coding, inductive pattern identification

---

## 1. Emergent Thematic Clusters

### T1: AGI Timeline Claims
Almost every person makes explicit predictions about AGI/ASI timelines:
- Jensen Huang: "within 5 years" (2024)
- Sam Altman: "confident we know how to build AGI" (2025)
- Elon Musk: "smarter than all humans combined by 2029"
- Masayoshi Son: "AGI in 2-3 years" (2025)
- Demis Hassabis: "ten times bigger than Industrial Revolution, ten times faster"

**Observation:** Timelines are becoming SHORTER over years. Earlier statements (2017-2020) speak of decades, later (2024-2026) of years. Clear candidate for timeline analysis (F2).

### T2: Democratization Rhetoric
Nearly universal — almost every person uses "democratize" or equivalents:
- Sundar Pichai: "ensure the AI divide doesn't become..."
- Jensen Huang: "codify the language of your culture"
- Sam Altman: "universal basic compute"
- Sergey Brin: "Technology is an inherent democratizer"
- Andrew Ng: "AI will transform every industry"
- Fei-Fei Li: Diversity in AI teams

**Observation:** Striking discrepancy between democratization narrative and actual power concentration. Same persons promising "democratization" control the infrastructure. Core finding for congruence analysis (F3).

### T3: Safety Ambivalence
Safety concerns are expressed but ALWAYS connected with continued building:
- Sam Altman: "worst fears... harm to the world" — keeps building
- Elon Musk: "more dangerous than nukes" — founds xAI
- Dario Amodei: "race between interpretability and intelligence" — scales Claude
- Jeff Dean: "risk of extinction... global priority" — stays at Google

**Observation:** No Top-20 actor slows building as consequence. Pattern is: "It's dangerous, THEREFORE we must build it (instead of others)." Systematic congruence pattern.

### T4: Self-Narrative as Historical Actor
Pronounced sense of mission:
- Sam Altman: addresses Trump, speaks of "new industry"
- Masayoshi Son: "born to realize ASI", "21st century Rothschild", 300-year plan
- Demis Hassabis: "waking up the universe"
- Larry Page: "If you're changing the world, you're working on important things"

**Observation:** Self-image oscillates between humble ("we don't know everything") and messianic ("born to realize ASI"). Dimension D01 (sense of mission) becomes highly relevant.

### T5: Open Source vs. Closed — the Zuckerberg Paradox
- Zuckerberg: "Open source is winning" + "1 billion downloads" BUT: "won't open source superintelligence"
- Elon Musk: Grok-1 open source, but also proprietary models
- Dario Amodei: Consciously closed-source for safety reasons

**Observation:** Open source functions as STRATEGIC tool, not conviction. Deployed when market advantages emerge (Llama against OpenAI), withdrawn when control becomes more important.

### T6: Human-AI Relationship — "Augmentation" as Standard Narrative
- Sundar Pichai: "augmenting human capabilities"
- Daniela Amodei: "vanishingly small" number of jobs without humans
- Tim Cook: "not first, but best for the customer"
- Andrew Ng: Cautious ("I don't think AGI is imminent")

**Observation:** "Augmentation" is the PR narrative. In the same breath, same persons speak of AGI that "smarter than all humans combined." Logical contradiction is not addressed.

### T7: Scale Language (Power as Metric)
Actions show clear pattern: Size as value in itself.
- Google: $3B into Anthropic, Amazon: $8B
- Meta: "multi-gigawatt" data centers
- xAI: $6B Series B
- Masayoshi Son: $100B Vision Fund

**Observation:** Investment sums grow exponentially. Language of power (billions, gigawatts, millions of users) pervades everything.

## 2. Surprises (Things NOT Expected)

### U1: Radical Honesty (Occasionally)
- Elon Musk: "Even if AI ultimately proves bad for humanity, I still want to be there to see it"
  → Curiosity over safety. Remarkably honest.
- Sam Altman: "I don't know what the percent chance is that I eventually get shot, but it's not zero"
  → Awareness of hostility toward tech leaders
- Larry Page: "I'd rather give my money to Elon Musk than to charity"
  → Open prioritization of tech entrepreneurs over traditional philanthropy

### U2: Biographical Legitimation
- Fei-Fei Li: "My parents worked in a dry cleaning shop"
- Tim Cook: "Being gay has given me a deeper understanding of what it means to be in the minority"
- Masayoshi Son: Korean-Japanese minority, 300-year plan

**Observation:** Personal origin narratives are deployed to legitimize universal claims. "I was myself disadvantaged, therefore I am authorized to speak for the disadvantaged."

### U3: Increasing Politicization (2024-2026)
- Sam Altman: $1M donation to Trump inauguration fund (after $200k to Biden)
- Altman: direct address to "Mr. President"
- Actions show increasing lobbying

**Observation:** Not expected in this clarity. AI elite positions itself actively in political arena — not just as advisors, but as actors.

## 4. Preliminary Hypotheses for Main Analysis

**H1:** Collective worldview of AI elite characterized by "constructive paradox": Simultaneously acknowledge existential threat AND present own work as solution.

**H2:** AGI-timeline compression (from decades to years) correlates with increasing commercial investment. Hypothesis: Financial interests drive optimistic predictions.

**H3:** Democratization narrative is functional, not descriptive. Serves to legitimize power concentration, not dissolve it.

**H4:** Increasing politicization (2024-2026) marks phase shift: From "Tech is apolitical" to "Tech IS politics".

**H5:** Sense of mission (D01) rises with power position. CEOs have higher D01 values than academics.
