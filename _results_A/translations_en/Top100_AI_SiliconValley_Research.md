# 1. METHODOLOGY

## 1.1 Research Question

Who are the 100 most influential people involved in the development or ownership of AI/LLM technologies and having a connection to Silicon Valley?

## 1.2 Operationalization of "Influence"

The term "influence" is operationalized using the following measurable criteria:

### Category A: Organizational Power (max. 10 points)
- CEO/President of top-10 AI company: +5 points
- CEO/President of top-20 AI company: +4 points
- Founder of top AI company: +4 points
- CTO/CSO/VP Engineering at top AI company: +3 points
- Board member of top AI company: +2 points
- Senior researcher/director at top AI company: +2 points
- Founder of AI startup (>$100M valuation): +2 points
- Founder of AI startup (<$100M valuation): +1 point

### Category B: Financial Influence (max. 6 points)
- AI-related wealth >$50 billion: +6 points
- AI-related wealth $10-50 billion: +5 points
- AI-related wealth $1-10 billion: +4 points
- AI-related wealth $100M-$1 billion: +3 points
- Major AI investor (VC, >$500M in AI): +3 points
- Significant AI investor (<$500M): +2 points

### Category C: Scientific Influence (max. 5 points)
- Turing Award or equivalent: +5 points
- h-Index >100 in AI/ML: +4 points
- Inventor of key algorithm (Transformer, etc.): +4 points
- >50 influential AI publications: +3 points
- Professorship at top university in AI: +2 points

### Category D: Public Visibility (max. 4 points)
- On TIME 100 AI list: +2 points
- On Forbes AI 50 (as leader): +2 points
- Regular media presence on AI: +1 point
- Keynote speaker at top AI conferences: +1 point

### Category E: Political/Regulatory Influence (max. 3 points)
- Advisor to US government on AI: +3 points
- Member of AI safety boards: +2 points
- Signatory of important AI statements: +1 point

### Category F: Source Verification (max. 5 points)
- Mentioned in 5+ independent sources: +5 points
- Mentioned in 4 independent sources: +4 points
- Mentioned in 3 independent sources: +3 points
- Mentioned in 2 independent sources: +2 points
- Mentioned in 1 source: +1 point

**Maximum Total Score: 33 Points**

## 1.3 Operationalization of "Silicon Valley Connection"

A person is considered "Silicon Valley connected" if at least one of the following criteria is met:

1. **Company headquarters:** Current or former company has/had headquarters in San Francisco Bay Area (San Francisco, San Jose, Palo Alto, Mountain View, Menlo Park, Cupertino, Sunnyvale, Santa Clara, Redwood City, etc.)
2. **Employer:** Current or former employment at SV company
3. **University:** Study, PhD, or professorship at Stanford University, UC Berkeley, or other Bay Area universities
4. **Residence:** Current or former residence in Bay Area
5. **Investments:** Active investments in SV-based AI companies (as VC or angel)

**Minimum requirement:** At least 1 criterion must be documentable.

## 1.5 Aggregation Method

1. **Collection:** All named persons from all sources collected (gross list)
2. **Deduplication:** Identical persons merged, multiple mentions increase source score
3. **SV Filter:** Only persons with documented Silicon Valley connection retained
4. **Scoring:** Each person rated according to defined system
5. **Ranking:** Sorted by total score, descending
6. **Cutoff:** Top 100 included in final list

## 1.6 Limitations (advance)

- Web search cannot capture all existing rankings
- Data currency depends on indexing
- Influence is multidimensional - all operationalizations are simplifications
- Silicon Valley connection may be hard to verify for some persons
- Bias toward people with high media presence
- Non-US persons (e.g., purely Chinese AI researchers) with SV connection may be underrepresented
