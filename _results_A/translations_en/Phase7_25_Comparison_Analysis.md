## 5. Surprises (across all comparisons)

1. **Anti-regulators as power-distributors:** GH_regcon_AH has the LOWEST D07 (2!) of all groups -- they want power neither with state nor firms, but with market/individual.

2. **Same risk score, opposite conclusions:** Risk-warners AND accelerators estimate catastrophe probability at 10-20%. The divergence lies NOT in risk assessment, but in value hierarchy.

3. **Top 10 = concentrated essence:** On 10/12 dimensions, exactly +1 vs. average. Power doesn't change thinking style, only its intensity.

4. **Women's worldview as corrective:** Women score higher on EVERY dimension associated with moderation (D04, D09, D11) and lower on EVERY dimension associated with extremism (D01, D02, D05, D07, D08).

5. **Academic voice at the margin:** Academics (6.3) are the most moderate type, but have least influence. Loudest voices (CEOs=7.8, investors=7.8) are most extreme.

6. **Rhetoric-action gap is SYSTEMATIC:** Not random, but on same dimensions (D04, D07, D11) -- responsibility, power, equality.

## 6. Findings for Research Questions

### F2 (Change): VG08
Worldview shifted from utopian techno-optimism (2010-2016) to tragic acceleration imperative (2023-2026). Rising responsibility + sinking control = tragic consciousness.

### F3 (Congruence): VG01
AI elite preaches democratization, practices concentration. Biggest gap: D11 (rhetoric=5, action=2), D07 (rhetoric=6, action=8).

### F5 (Differences): VG02-VG07
Three strongest predictors: (1) Stance (Risk/Speed, Delta 1.7), (2) Role (CEO/Academic, Delta 1.5), (3) Gender (Delta 1.2). Firms (Delta 0.4) and regulation stance (Delta 0.5) differentiate less.

### F6 (Power): VG10
Power intensifies worldview but doesn't change it qualitatively. Top 10 = "concentrated essence" of collective.
