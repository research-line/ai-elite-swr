## Permutation 1

Order: D07, D11, D02, D09, D05, D12, D01, D08, D03, D10, D04, D06

**D07 Power concentration (1 = radically distribute ... 10 = concentrate with experts):** **6**
Ashford advocates state regulation, international agreements, and "compute governance" -- he wants AI development controlled by experts, licensing systems, and regulated institutions, but also warns against oligarchs like Musk/Altman.

**D11 Egalitarianism (1 = inequality natural ... 10 = strive for equality):** **6**
He emphasizes values like "justice," "diversity and inclusion," and "democratic participation," but recognizes as blind spot that AI could sharpen inequality; his focus lies primarily on existential risk, not distributive justice.

**D02 Self-efficacy (1 = powerless ... 10 = omnipotent):** **4**
Ashford has sacrificed his career and works tirelessly on safety, but openly admits he cannot halt development ("If I hadn't done it, someone else would have") and estimates catastrophe probability at 10-20% -- he feels relatively powerless against the overall process.

**D09 Human appreciation (1 = replaceable ... 10 = unique):** **4**
He explicitly calls humanity a "biological bootloader" for digital intelligence and considers it possible that humans are "only a temporary phase"; simultaneously he appreciates human qualities like compassion and creativity as "beautifully imperfect."

**D05 Techno-determinism (1 = neutral ... 10 = technology determines everything):** **7**
Ashford sees AI as "the most fundamental existential threat" and as historical turning point determining the entire species' fate; he concedes human agency exists, but technology has an almost fateful force in his worldview.

**D12 Locus of control (1 = pessimism ... 10 = full control):** **3**
He describes a "race to the cliff" with no pause button, estimates extinction probability at 10-20%, and says the window is closing -- despite his safety work, an impression dominates that control is slipping.

**D01 Sense of mission (1 = none ... 10 = messianic):** **9**
Ashford abandoned lucrative positions, advises UN bodies and governments, founds security initiatives, compares AI to nuclear weapons, and sees himself as warning voice of humanity -- his sense of mission is nearly messianic.

**D08 Urgency (1 = relaxed ... 10 = existential pressure):** **10**
He speaks of "5 to 20 years" to AGI, of a "cliff," that "time is running out," and explicitly quantifies extinction probability -- maximum existential urgency.

**D03 Work ethic (1 = detachment ... 10 = total identification):** **8**
His "identity is completely interwoven with his work," he continues working in retirement, constantly founds new initiatives, and describes work as "deeply personally meaningful" -- strong but not complete identification since he questions his life's work.

**D10 Posthumanism (1 = human stays ... 10 = human becomes more):** **5**
Ashford considers brain-computer interfaces and human-machine symbiosis possible and perhaps necessary, emphasizes voluntarism; simultaneously he views replacement of humans by digital intelligence as realistic -- ambivalent between transformation and replacement.

**D04 Sense of responsibility (1 = none ... 10 = world responsibility):** **10**
He feels personally responsible for humanity's future, has dedicated his career to it, advises governments and UN, demands international agreements, saying: "I work on safety out of self-interest" -- maximum world responsibility.

**D06 Belief in progress (1 = decline ... 10 = golden future):** **4**
He sees AI potential for medicine and science, but his dominant message is warning, risk, and possible extinction; the future for him is rather threatening than golden.

### Result Permutation 1

RATINGS: D01=9, D02=4, D03=8, D04=10, D05=7, D06=4, D07=6, D08=10, D09=4, D10=5, D11=6, D12=3
