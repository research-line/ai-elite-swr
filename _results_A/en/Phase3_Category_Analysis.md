## 1. Sample Selection and Justification

### Selection Logic

The sample (n=17 of 100) was stratified along three axes:

**A) Rating Extrema (Top-5 and Bottom-5 by Avg Score)**
- Ensure that both the most prominent and most moderate worldviews are included in the analysis.

**B) Profile Divergences (moderate Avg, but extreme individual dimensions)**
- Capture individuals who are unremarkable on average but strongly deviate in specific dimensions.

**C) Demographic Diversity**
- Inclusion of all available women and non-Western backgrounds.

## 2. Identified New Themes (not covered by existing 12 categories)

### NT-1: Epistemic Self-Understanding / Knowledge Claims

**Definition:** Statements about what can be known, the certainty of predictions, and how one's own expertise legitimizes the knowledge claim.

**Examples from Sample:**
- P30 (Volger): "Nobody really understands what's happening in these models. But that doesn't stop us from using them." -- Framing uncertainty as acceptable
- P59 (Bernhardt): "I heard that already in the 1980s, and it was just as laughable then." -- Historical knowledge as basis for skepticism
- P60 (Kornelius): "I say it because I know the mathematics, because I understand the limitations." -- Professional competence as legitimation
- P21 (Reinhardt): "50 percent chance in 5 to 20 years" -- Quantified uncertainty as rhetorical instrument
- P16 (Tanaka): No reflection on limits of own knowledge despite massive predictions

**Demarcation:** This theme does not fall under SB (Self-Image) because it's not about identity, but about epistemic positioning. It does not fall under ET (Ethics) because it's not a value question, but a knowledge question. It does not fall under FO (Progress) because it's not about evaluating progress, but about the foundation on which judgments are made.

**Recommendation:** Introduce new primary category EP (Epistemics/Knowledge Claim).

---

### NT-2: Democratization Narrative vs. Power Concentration (Congruence Problem)

**Definition:** Statements that explicitly or implicitly address or reproduce the tension between proclaimed democratization and actual power concentration.

**Examples:**
- P5 (Velker): "Open source is winning" + "won't open source superintelligence" -- Explicit contradiction in one person
- P48 (Montfort): "Technology cannot remain in the hands of a few" -- CEO of a $4.5B company with Big Tech partnerships
- P95 (Mansouri): "You can't beat centralized AI with more centralized AI" -- Builds unicorn himself
- P50 (Hartwell): Propagating decentralization as VC partner with billions in AUM
- P3 (Stellaris): Calls for regulation, builds largest compute infrastructures

**Demarcation:** This theme is currently partially captured by three categories simultaneously (GE, MA, RE), but none focuses explicitly on the CONTRADICTION between rhetoric and action. The Phase 2 memo recommends a "congruence marker."

**Recommendation:** Introduce not as its own primary category, but as a new secondary marker KON (Congruence/Incongruence). This marks statements in which a person either reproduces (unconsciously), addresses (consciously), or resolves a contradiction between their own position and own action. The marker can be assigned to any primary category.

---

### NT-3: Temporality / Timeline Rhetoric

**Definition:** Statements that contain explicit time predictions, reveal the thinking time horizon, or employ urgency as a rhetorical means.

**Examples:**
- P16 (Tanaka): 300-year plan AND "AGI in 2-3 years" -- Double temporality
- P85 (Reinhardt): "We'll see it in our lifetime" -- Lifetime as horizon
- P88 (Wegener): "Months, not decades" -- Compression
- P57 (Brenner): "AGI between 2026 and 2035" -- Quantified timeline
- P59 (Bernhardt): "I don't see it in the near future" -- Anti-timeline
- P60 (Kornelius): "The AI revolution hasn't happened yet" -- Counter-narrative

**Demarcation:** Temporality is currently distributed across FO (Progress), RI (Risk), and WV (Worldview). However, timeline rhetoric is an independent phenomenon: the same person can be simultaneously optimistic and pessimistic, but their timeline statement stands on its own as an empirical claim with rhetorical function. D08 (Urgency) in the rating captures the feeling, but not the specific prediction.

**Recommendation:** No new primary category, but introduce new secondary marker ZP (Time Prediction/Timeline) that specifically marks statements with explicit time specifications and prepares for time series analysis (F2).

---

### NT-4: Biographical Legitimation

**Definition:** Statements in which personal experiences (origin, suffering, failure, minority status) are used to legitimize universal claims.

**Examples:**
- P95 (Mansouri): Son's autism diagnosis as starting point for universal AI democratization
- P16 (Tanaka): "I was born to realize ASI" -- Biography as teleology
- P64 (Chen): Thoreau reference as legitimation for contrary position
- P88 (Wegener): CERN experience as legitimation for "What is mind?" question
- P59 (Bernhardt): Academic career as legitimation for skepticism

**Demarcation:** Partially falls under SB (Self-Image), but SB captures IDENTITY, not the rhetorical USE of biography. This function is systematic and deserves its own coding.

**Recommendation:** Introduce new secondary marker BIO (Biographical Legitimation).

---

### NT-5: Ecological Blindness / Materiality

**Definition:** The systematic ABSENCE of statements about ecological costs, energy consumption, material foundations of technology.

**Examples from Blind Spot Analyses:**
- P16 (Tanaka): Not a single word about energy consumption despite "500 billion dollar infrastructure"
- P85 (Reinhardt): No mention of ecological costs of "10 billion robots"
- P5 (Velker): "Multi-gigawatt data centers" without mention of ecological consequences
- P88 (Wegener): "100,000 GPUs in 122 days" -- pure performance narrative

**Demarcation:** This is a relevant coding problem: the ABSENCE of a topic cannot be directly coded since it concerns non-statements. Nevertheless, the systematic absence is a finding.

**Recommendation:** No separate category, but document in the coding manual as a systematic finding. For the few statements that DO MENTION ecology (Phase 2 memo identifies only Lisa Su), the existing category GE (Society) or a subcategory of RI (Risk) can be used.

## 3. Evaluation of Each Existing Primary Category

### WV -- Worldview
**Status: RETAIN, but clarify**

The category is central and is addressed in every synthesis. However, it is currently TOO BROAD: It encompasses both the overall perspective ("The world is undergoing the greatest transformation") and more specific societal diagnoses. Recommendation: Restrict WV to the OVERARCHING future vision, assign specific diagnoses to other categories.

**Demarcation Problem:** WV overlaps massively with FO (Progress) and GE (Society). Solution: WV = the overall narrative level ("where is the world going?"), FO = evaluation of progress ("is that good/bad/inevitable?"), GE = consequences for society ("what does it mean for people?").

---

### SB -- Self-Image
**Status: RETAIN**

Well-demarcated and clearly identifiable in every synthesis. The sample shows clear bandwidth: from "I'm not a visionary, I'm pragmatic" (P64) through "I'm someone who learns" (P69) to "I was born to realize ASI" (P16).

**No change needed.** Biographical legitimation (NT-4) supplements SB as a secondary marker but does not overlap.

---

### MB -- Human Image
**Status: RETAIN, check TR (see section 4)**

The category captures statements about human nature. Examples:
- P3 (Stellaris): "The human is the biological bootloader for AI"
- P57 (Brenner): "Humans have compassion -- machines don't"
- P60 (Kornelius): "Human intelligence is distributed, not individual"
- P77 (Weidner): "Humans just want their problems solved"

Functioning well, no change needed -- provided TR question is resolved.

---

### ET -- Ethics/Values
**Status: RETAIN, but demarcate sharply from RE**

ET and RE (Regulation) overlap in statements like "We need an AI arbitrator" (P3) or "Safety must be a condition for business" (P57). Recommendation: ET = values and principles ("openness is ethically right"), RE = concrete regulation proposals and demands ("we need kill-switches").

---

### GE -- Society
**Status: RETAIN, but consider subcategories**

GE is the broadest category and currently captures:
- Work/employment (already has its own category AR)
- Inequality/distribution
- Democracy/participation
- Education
- Health

The overlap with AR is problematic. Recommendation: Restrict GE to NON-ECONOMIC societal questions (democracy, education, health, inequality). AR remains responsible for economic questions.

---

### RI -- Risk/Security
**Status: RETAIN**

Central category with clear application. Bandwidth from "Not yet" (P30) through "10-20% risk" (P3) to "existential threat" (P21, P57). Well-demarcated.

**Consider Subcategorization:**
- RI-EX: Existential risks (extinction, loss of control)
- RI-GV: Governance risks (power concentration, lack of regulation)
- RI-SO: Social risks (deepfakes, manipulation, job loss)

---

### FO -- Progress/Acceleration
**Status: RETAIN, sharpen demarcation from WV**

Captures the EVALUATION of technological progress (good/bad/inevitable/steerable). Examples:
- P85 (Reinhardt): "Speed is moral necessity"
- P59 (Bernhardt): "Remarkable, but not unprecedented"
- P60 (Kornelius): "Hype cycle, bubble"
- P64 (Chen): "Whoever leads wins. Whoever falls behind loses."

The demarcation from WV must become clearer (see WV entry above).

---

### MA -- Power/Control
**Status: RETAIN**

Central and well-applicable. Captures explicit statements about power relations:
- P48 (Montfort): "Power concentration at few major tech companies"
- P50 (Hartwell): "Who controls the network?"
- P21 (Reinhardt): "Oligarchs with power nobody should have"
- P95 (Mansouri): "Blocking technology is violence"

**No change needed.** The congruence marker (KON) supplements MA with the tension between proclaimed and actual power.

---

### AR -- Work/Economy
**Status: RETAIN, demarcate sharply from GE**

AR should capture ONLY economic statements:
- Labor market, job loss, retraining
- Economic structure, business models
- Investments, capital, value creation
- P95 (Mansouri): "The entire concept of money and value is being questioned"
- P64 (Chen): "The best products always win"

Non-economic societal issues belong to GE.

---

### TR -- Transhumanism
**Status: RETAIN INDEPENDENTLY (Result of Review)**

**Analysis of Subsumability under MB:**

The Phase 2 memo asks whether TR can be subsumed under MB. After analyzing the 17 syntheses: NO. The reasoning:

1. **TR has its own content logic:** TR statements concern not what humans ARE, but what humans CAN/SHOULD/WILL BECOME. P3 (Stellaris) illustrates the difference: "The human is a fascinating being" (MB) vs. "We are the biological bootloader for AI" (TR). The former describes, the latter projects.

2. **TR is the dimension with greatest variance (D10: range 2-9):** This shows that TR is the most differentiating characteristic across the sample. Subsuming it under MB would eliminate the very dimension that distinguishes most between people.

3. **Sample Evidence for Independent TR Statements:**
   - P16 (Tanaka): Telepathy through brain chips, humans and ASI as partners, 10 billion robots
   - P85 (Reinhardt): "Creating a new species", Von Neumann probes
   - P3 (Stellaris): Human-machine symbiosis as only viable path
   - P59 (Bernhardt): NO TR content -- conscious rejection of this dimension
   - P60 (Kornelius): D10=2, explicit anti-TR position ("Humans remain")
   - P21 (Reinhardt): "Humanity could be only a temporary phase" -- TR as warning

4. **TR and MB are COMPLEMENTARY, not redundant:** MB says what humans are. TR says what humans should become. This complementarity is analytically valuable and would be lost through subsumption.

**Recommendation:** TR remains independent primary category. Refine definition as: "Statements about human transformation, extension, or transcendence through technology -- both affirmatively and critically."

---

### RE -- Regulation
**Status: RETAIN, demarcate sharply from ET**

RE captures concrete regulation statements:
- P57 (Brenner): "Kill-switches in all advanced systems"
- P3 (Stellaris): "We need an AI referee"
- P21 (Reinhardt): "SB 1047 as absolute minimum"
- P48 (Montfort): "Weaponizing fear to justify entrenchment"

Demarcation from ET: RE = concrete governance mechanisms and demands. ET = overarching value principles.

---

### SP -- Spirituality/Meaning
**Status: RETAIN, but EXPAND to SP/EX (Spirituality/Existential)**

SP currently captures too narrowly only explicit spiritual/religious statements. The sample shows a broader existential dimension:
- P3 (Stellaris): "What is consciousness?" / simulation hypothesis
- P16 (Tanaka): "I have only one faith: singularity" -- quasi-religious language
- P30 (Volger): "Divine benevolence" as explanation for AI functionality
- P57 (Brenner): "Greatest event in human history"
- P69 (Vaswani): "There is always more to learn" -- existential attitude toward knowledge seeking

Recommendation: Rename SP to SP/EX and define as: "Statements with existential, spiritual, or quasi-religious content, including meaning questions, consciousness, transcendence, and teleological interpretation of one's own role."

## 4. Overlap Problems and Solutions

### Problem 1: WV vs. FO vs. GE (Triple-Overlap)

**Symptom:** A statement like "The world is undergoing the greatest transformation" (common) could be coded under WV, FO, or GE.

**Solution -- Hierarchical Principle:**
- WV = Overall narrative level ("Where is the world heading overall?")
- FO = Evaluation of progress ("Is that good/bad/steerable/inevitable?")
- GE = Specific societal consequences ("What does that mean for democracy, education, health?")

**Coding Rule:** Give statements the MOST SPECIFIC category. "The world is transforming" = WV. "The transformation is good" = FO. "The transformation will worsen inequality" = GE.

---

### Problem 2: ET vs. RE (Values vs. Regulation)

**Symptom:** "Openness is ethically right AND should be promoted through regulation" -- two categories in one statement.

**Solution -- Dual-Coding:**
Statements containing both a value stance and a regulation demand are coded DUAL (ET + RE). Explicitly allow in the coding manual that one statement can receive up to 2 primary categories.

---

### Problem 3: GE vs. AR (Society vs. Work/Economy)

**Symptom:** "Jobs will disappear and profits land with the rich" -- economic and societal simultaneously.

**Solution -- Primary Rule:**
AR takes precedence for ECONOMIC statements (jobs, capital, business models). GE takes precedence for NON-ECONOMIC societal statements (democracy, education, social division). For hybrid statements: Primary AR if the focus is on economic mechanisms, primary GE if the focus is on social consequences.

---

### Problem 4: SB vs. EP (Self-Image vs. Epistemics)

**Symptom:** "I see patterns before others see them" -- Self-Image OR epistemic claim?

**Solution -- Intention Rule:**
If the statement primarily describes IDENTITY ("I am someone who..."), then SB. If the statement primarily makes a KNOWLEDGE CLAIM ("I know/understand/see what others don't"), then EP. The new category EP specifically captures epistemic POSITIONING, not identity construction.

## 7. Specific Demarcation Problems with Solutions

### 7.1 Where does "Open Source" belong?

Open source discourse is coded differently depending on context:
- "Open source is ethically right" = ET
- "Open source prevents power concentration" = MA
- "Open source as business model" = AR
- "Open source as regulatory approach" = RE
- "Open source democratizes AI for all" = GE

**Coding Rule:** Identify the PRIMARY framework of the statement. Open source is an INSTRUMENT, not a TOPIC. It is coded under the topic it serves.

### 7.2 Where does "AGI comes in X years" belong?

- Primary: FO (Progress evaluation)
- Secondary: ZP (Timeline marker)
- NOT: WV (unless the statement is part of overall narrative)
- NOT: RI (unless time specification is explicitly linked to risk assessment)

### 7.3 Where does the "bootloader" metaphor belong?

P3 (Stellaris): "The human is the biological bootloader for AI"
- Primary: TR (Transformation/human transcendence)
- NOT: MB (because the statement doesn't describe what the human IS, but what they BECOME in cosmic context)

### 7.4 Where do investment statements belong?

"I invest 500 billion dollars in AI infrastructure" (P16)
- Primary: AR (Economy/investment)
- Secondary: HA (Action reference), STR (Strategic)
- NOT: FO or WV (unless investment is explicitly framed as contribution to progress or worldview)

## 8. Response to Phase 2 Memo Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: "Add congruence marker"
**Implemented:** KON as new secondary marker (see section 5).

### Recommendation 2: "Political positioning as separate subcategory"
**Partially implemented:** POL (Political) remains secondary marker, but is CLARIFIED:
- POL marks statements with explicit political content (parties, governments, lobbying, campaigns)
- The "increasing politicization" identified in Phase 2 memo (H4) becomes systematically capturable through combination of primary category + POL marker
- Example: P5 (Velker) $1M to Trump = AR+POL+HA; P57 (Brenner) before Senate committee = RE+POL

A separate PRIMARY category "Politics" is not needed since political positioning appears as MODE in every primary category.

### Recommendation 3: "Check if TR must remain independent"
**Checked and answered:** TR remains independent (see section 3, TR entry, with detailed reasoning).
