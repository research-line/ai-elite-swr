## 1. CORE RESEARCH QUESTIONS

### F1: WORLDVIEW (descriptive)
**What does the AI elite think about itself, the world, and humanity?**

Sub-questions:
- F1a: What is the collective worldview of all 100?
- F1b: What is each individual person's worldview?
- F1c: What is the worldview of the powerful top tier (top 10)?
- F1d: What are the core beliefs (most frequently repeated statements)?

### F2: CHANGE (longitudinal)
**How has the AI elite's worldview changed from 2010 to 2026?**

Sub-questions:
- F2a: How do the 12 worldview dimensions change across years?
- F2b: Are there tipping points (breakpoints), e.g., after ChatGPT?
- F2c: Do worldviews converge or diverge over time?
- F2d: Do dominant metaphors and emotions change?

### F3: CONGRUENCE (validation)
**Do the AI elite's statements and actions agree?**

Sub-questions:
- F3a: Do statements and actions produce the same worldview?
- F3b: How does congruence develop over time?
- F3c: Does congruent differ from incongruent persons' worldviews?
- F3d: Can actions be predicted from statements and vice versa?

### F4: CLUSTER (exploratory)
**What worldview types exist in the AI elite, and who belongs where?**

Sub-questions:
- F4a: What natural clusters form from statements?
- F4b: What natural clusters form from actions?
- F4c: Do worldview clusters correlate with real-world proximity (same company, network)?
- F4d: Which persons are bridges, which isolated?

### F5: DIFFERENCE (comparative)
**Which AI elite subgroups think differently than others?**

Sub-questions:
- F5a: CEOs vs. academics vs. investors vs. founders?
- F5b: Open source vs. closed source?
- F5c: Risk warners vs. accelerators?
- F5d: Company cultures: Anthropic vs. OpenAI vs. Google?
- F5e: Gender differences?
- F5f: Regulation advocates vs. opponents?

### F6: POWER (normative)
**Which worldview has the most power and resources?**

Sub-questions:
- F6a: Which worldview cluster controls the most resources?
- F6b: Does the worldview of the powerful (top 10) match industry average?

## 2. REDUCTION CRITERIA (for methods section)

From the theoretical combination space of ~1,227 vessels, the research design
is reduced to what is necessary by the following criteria:

### C1: RESEARCH QUESTION BINDING (mandatory)
Each vessel must be assigned to AT LEAST ONE research question (F1-F6).
Vessels without research question connection are excluded.

### C2: MINIMUM VESSEL SIZE (mandatory)
A vessel must contain >= 15 data points (statements + actions) to enable
stable synthesis. Vessels with fewer data points are excluded or merged
with adjacent vessels.

### C3: REDUNDANCY BAN (mandatory)
If vessel A is a true subset of vessel B AND both answer the same research
question, the redundant vessel is excluded.
Example: "Top-10 annual vessel 2022" is subset of "all annual vessel 2022"
and answers the same question (F2) -> top-10 annual vessel dropped.

### C4: MODALITY ECONOMY (principle)
Separation into statements-only (S) and actions-only (A) occurs ONLY when
a research question requires separate analysis:
- F3 (congruence) requires S/A separation -> S and A separate
- F1, F2, F5 do NOT require separation -> S+A only (combined)

### C5: CLARITY CONSTRAINT (guideline)
Vessels should be interpretively transparent. Overly complex filter combinations
(e.g., "women only, top 20 only, CEOs only, statements only, 2019 only") are
reduced to broader categories unless the research question demands specificity.

## 3. REDUCTION MATRIX: FROM THEORETICALLY POSSIBLE TO ACTUALLY NEEDED

### Theoretical Space:
- ~1,227 possible combinations (Level 1-4)
- After applying C1-C5: ~250 practically realizable vessels
- Recommended realization: ~210 vessels (P1 priority)

### Applied Reductions:

**A) Modality Reduction by Research Question:**

| Research Question | Modality Handling | Reasoning |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| F1 (Worldview) | S+A combined | Full picture needed |
| F2 (Change) | S+A combined | Temporal comparison needs full data |
| F3 (Congruence) | S separate, A separate, S+A combined | Explicitly compares modalities |
| F4 (Cluster) | S+A combined | Type formation needs full data |
| F5 (Difference) | S+A combined | Group comparison needs full data |
| F6 (Power) | Uses F4/F5 clusters | No new vessels needed |

**Result:** Instead of ~400 vessels if all modalities split for all questions,
reduce to ~210 by combining where possible.

**B) Person Reduction by Research Question:**

| Research Question | Person Handling | Vessels Needed |
|-----------------|-----------------|------------------|
| F1 (Worldview) | All 100, top 10, top 20 separate | 23 vessels |
| F2 (Change) | All 100 only | 17 annual + 9 epoch = 26 vessels |
| F3 (Congruence) | Top 20 individual, all 100 | ~40 vessels |
| F4 (Cluster) | Clusters emergent from all 100 | ~65 vessels |
| F5 (Difference) | Pre-defined groups (CEO, academic, etc.) | ~30 vessels |
| F6 (Power) | Uses F4 results | 0 new vessels |

**Result:** Instead of 300 individual vessels if rating each person separately,
reduce to ~100 by selective focus on top individuals and group aggregates.

**C) Time Reduction by Research Question:**

| Research Question | Time Handling | Vessels |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| F1 (Worldview) | Overall period only | No time split |
| F2 (Change) | All 17 years + 3 epochs | 17+3 = 20 vessels |
| F3 (Congruence) | Select breakpoint years | ~5 vessels |
| F4 (Cluster) | Overall + selective epochs | ~8 vessels |
| F5 (Difference) | Overall period | No time split |
| F6 (Power) | Overall + optional temporal contrast | 1-2 vessels |

**Result:** Instead of ~51 annual vessels if creating for all person groups,
reduce to ~20 by creating year-by-year analysis only for F2.

## 6. PAPER METHODOLOGY TEXT (DRAFT)

The study employs a data reduction strategy to transform theoretical combination space (~1,227 possible analyses) into a practical, theory-driven research design. Six core research questions (F1-F6) guide vessel selection. Each vessel combines exactly one independent variable combination (person, modality, time) with one dependent variable (worldview synthesis). Vessels are reduced by three principles: research question binding, minimum sample size (n≥15), and modality economy. The final design comprises 210 synthesis units of P1 priority and an additional 80 P2 units for exploratory analysis. This reduces the theoretical space by 83% while preserving coverage of all core research questions.
