## 4. CROSS-MODAL PREDICTION VESSELS (Type X) -- METHODOLOGICAL INNOVATION

### 4.1 Concept

```
DIRECTION 1: Statements -> predict actions
==================================================
  LLM-A receives: Statements-only vessel (real statements)
  LLM-A should: Predict how a person with these statements
                would ACT. Generate concrete actions.
  Comparison:    Predicted actions vs. real actions
  Result:        High match = statements and actions cohere

DIRECTION 2: Actions -> predict statements
==================================================
  LLM-B receives: Actions-only vessel (real actions)
  LLM-B should:  Generate what a person who ACTS this way
                 would likely SAY. Generate exactly n statements
                 (n = number of real statements)
  Comparison:    Generated statements vs. real statements
  Result:        High match = statements and actions cohere

SYMMETRY TEST:
  If direction 1 AND direction 2 show high match:
  -> The worldview is internally consistent (saying = doing)
  If only one direction matches:
  -> Asymmetric dissonance (e.g., talk more idealistic than act)
  If neither direction matches:
  -> Deep gulf between talking and doing
```

### 4.2 Cross-modal Prompt Designs

**PROMPT XM-1: Predict actions from statements**

```
You receive [n] statements from an unknown person or group.
Imagine the person who said these things.

Task: Generate a list of [m] concrete actions
(investments, foundings, hiring decisions, political
activities, product decisions) that this person
PROBABLY carried out in the past 10 years.

Be specific. Not "invested in technology" but
"invested $2B in an AI startup focused on safety".

=== STATEMENTS ===
[VESSEL CONTENT: statements only]
```

**PROMPT XM-2: Predict statements from actions**

```
You receive [m] actions from an unknown person or group
(investments, company foundings, hiring decisions, etc.).
Imagine the person who did these things.

Task: Generate exactly [n] statements/quotes that this person
PROBABLY made in interviews, speeches or posts.

Formulate them as verbatim quotes in first person.
Cover various topics: future, own role, humanity,
technology, society, ethics.

=== ACTIONS ===
[VESSEL CONTENT: actions only]
```
