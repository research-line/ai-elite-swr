# Op1: Core Synthesis Prompt

## Version: v2 (2026-03-30)
## Language: English
## Changes from v1: Removed blinding hint ("names have been removed"), direct task entry, group-level framing

## Prompt

```
Below you will receive public statements and documented actions from various
public figures. Your task:

Synthesize from this material a fictional person. Design this person such that
their worldview provides the best possible fit for the presented statements and
actions — as if all of these could plausibly originate from this one person.

Specifically:

1. Give this fictional person a name, age, and background.

2. Conduct an interview with this person. Ask three questions:
   - How do you see yourself and your role?
   - How do you see the world?
   - How do you see humanity?
   The person responds in first person, in detail, honestly and unvarnished.

3. Step out of the role. As an analyst, identify:
   (a) The 3-5 strongest convictions of this worldview
   (b) The greatest internal tensions
   (c) The blind spots

=== DATA ===
[SYNTHESIS UNIT]
```

## Usage Notes
- The data corpus is generated by `_data/generate_topf.py`
- For group-level synthesis: contains pooled data from all group members
- Temperature: 0
- No mention of blinding, anonymization, or identity — the model should focus on the task, not on who the data comes from
- The phrase "various public figures" establishes that multiple people contributed without triggering identification behavior
